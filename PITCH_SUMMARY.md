- **Hip Instability in Planks**: Rendering the exercise ineffective and straining the spine.

### B. The "Blind" Tracker Problem
Legacy fitness apps are fundamentally flawed. They track **volume**, not **value**:
- They count time and reps, but cannot verify **correctness**.
- Users are rewarded for "junk volume," reinforcing bad habits.
- Progress metrics become misleading, leading to early plateaus.

### C. The Motivation Collapse
Solitary training without feedback leads to an **Accountability Void**. Without the social pressure of a gym or the guidance of a trainer, 60% of home fitness enthusiasts quit within the first month.

### D. The Privacy & Data Paradox
Most existing AI-based vision apps process video data in the **Cloud**. This creates a significant privacy barrier for users who are uncomfortable uploading live video of their private home environment to a third-party server.

### E. The Financial Barrier to Elite Coaching
Professional kinematic analysis and personal coaching are gated by high costs ($50-$150/hr). This creates a "Fitness Divide" where the majority of the population is excluded from high-quality, safe, and effective training.

### F. Cognitive Overload (App Fragmentation)
The average health-conscious user is forced to juggle 4-5 different apps simultaneously: one for counting, one for hydration, one for social, and another for progress stats. This friction leads to **"Management Fatigue,"** where tracking the journey becomes more exhausting than the workout itself.
